1.Potala

The Potala Palace in Lhasa, Tibet, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and former residence of the Dalai Lama. Famous for its grand architecture, it houses golden stupas, sacred chapels, vast murals, and thousands of Buddhist relics. Rising majestically above the city, the palace symbolizes Tibet’s rich history, religion, and culture.

2.Jokhang Temple

The Jokhang Temple in Lhasa, Tibet, is the holiest site of Tibetan Buddhism and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Built in the 7th century, it houses the revered Jowo Shakyamuni statue, intricate murals, and ancient Buddhist relics. Surrounded by the Barkhor Street pilgrimage circuit, the temple is a spiritual and cultural heart of Tibet.

3.Tashi Lhunpo Monastery

Tashi Lhunpo Monastery in Shigatse, Tibet, founded in 1447, is the seat of the Panchen Lama. Known for its giant Maitreya Buddha, golden stupas, and sacred halls, it reflects Tibet’s deep spiritual and cultural heritage.

4. Sera Monastery

Sera Monastery, one of Tibet’s great Gelug university monasteries, is famous for its lively monk debates held in the debating courtyard, where monks discuss Buddhist philosophy with energy and clapping gestures. Visitors are drawn to its ancient assembly hall, beautiful murals, and numerous chapels filled with statues of the Buddha and past Dalai Lamas. The peaceful atmosphere, surrounded by mountains, and the rhythmic chanting of monks make Sera Monastery a captivating spiritual and cultural attraction.
